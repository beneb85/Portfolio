The Approach

Art direction for journalism is a discipline of restraint. The visual language must serve the story — never compete with it. Across editorial, social, and print formats, the challenge is always the same: how does the eye enter the page, and where does it go next?

Key considerations across this work:

  • Typography as a structural element, not just a carrier of text
  • Colour used for hierarchy and emotion, not decoration
  • Data visualisations that read as editorial content, not charts
  • Consistent visual identity across wildly different formats and contexts

The Work

This collection spans three years of editorial and visual work — magazine features, social media campaigns, award-winning infographic series, and print campaigns for cultural and political subjects.

Recurring formats include:

  • Long-form magazine layouts with integrated data graphics
  • Social media design systems for ongoing editorial series
  • Print infographics combining typography, illustration, and data
  • Motion graphics for short-form digital distribution

Each project is driven by a single question: what is the most direct visual path to understanding?
